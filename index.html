Frequently Asked Questions

A VPN, or virtual private network, is a tool that protects your online activity from spying eyes. It encrypts your data and routes it through a secure tunnel.

There are several types of VPNs, including remote access VPNs, site-to-site VPNs, and client-based VPNs. Each serves a different purpose depending on the needs of the user, whether it's individual security or connecting multiple networks.

A VPN helps you protect your privacy and security while online by encrypting your internet connection, hiding your IP address, and providing access to restricted content.

In most countries, VPNs are legal to use. However, some countries restrict VPN usage, especially for bypassing government censorship or engaging in illegal activities.
